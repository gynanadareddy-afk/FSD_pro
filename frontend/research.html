Research Overview

Excellence in Computer Science Research

The Department of Computer Science and Engineering is committed to advancing the frontiers of technology through cutting-edge research and innovation. Our research programs span across multiple domains of computer science, addressing real-world challenges and contributing to the global knowledge base.

With state-of-the-art laboratories and a team of renowned faculty members, we foster an environment that encourages interdisciplinary collaboration and breakthrough discoveries. Our research initiatives are supported by various funding agencies and industry partnerships.

Research Cutoffs

Admission Requirements for Research Programs

Ph.D. Program

Minimum Qualification: M.Tech/M.E/M.S in CSE or equivalent
Minimum GATE Score: 350-400 (General), 300-350 (OBC/SC/ST)
NET/SET Qualification: Qualified candidates eligible
Selection Process: Written Test + Interview

M.Tech Research

Minimum Qualification: B.Tech/B.E in CSE or equivalent
Minimum GATE Score: 300-350 (General), 250-300 (OBC/SC/ST)
Work Experience: Preferred but not mandatory
Selection Process: GATE Score + Interview

Note: The cutoff scores mentioned above are indicative and may vary based on the number of applicants and seat availability. Final selection is based on combined performance in qualifying examination and personal interview.
